# Wikipoell

Wikipoell is an archive-focused website dedicated to documenting the work of **Carol Christian Poell**.

## Why this exists

### Why it makes sense to categorize Poell’s work

Much of Poell’s output is intensely categorical: each garment is tied to a system of identifiers—materials, color, silhouette, treatments, processes, and special details are often encoded consistently and repeat across seasons. Color codes (for example color 12 being dark green), fabric naming conventions, and other shared vocabularies run through the line. That structure is perfect for storing and querying in a deliberate way rather than scattering notes across bookmarks and vague image searches.

### Where the idea came from

I started this partly from a selfish question—whether combat boots ever appeared in color 12. CCP is a niche brand and the open web barely answers “needle in a haystack” questions like that. A dedicated archive lets you narrow by concrete parameters instead of guessing keywords.

### What opens up beyond one-offs

Beyond one-off hunts, it’s useful for cross-cutting curiosity: examples like **any blue pieces in kangaroo leather**, or **footwear with prosthetic insert and rubber drips**, become answerable once pieces are modeled with stable fields and filters instead of lone forum posts.

### Aim

The goal is to make Poell’s naming and categorization usable for collectors and researchers: precise lookups, repeatable answers, and a single place to explore garments.
